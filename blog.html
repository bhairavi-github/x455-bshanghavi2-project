When choosing an outfit for a pet photography photoshoot, it's important to keep a few things in mind:

Comfort: Wear something Comfortable.

Color: Choose colors that will complement your pet's coat. Solid colors, pastels, and earth tones tend to work well.

Texture: Avoid anything too shiny or sparkly. Instead, choose fabrics with a matte finish.

Practicality: Make sure your outfit is practical and allows for movement.

Yes, you should definitely consider bringing some treats to your pet photoshoot. Treats can be a great way to keep your pet focused, engaged, and well-behaved during the shoot. They can also help to reinforce good behavior and reward your pet for a job well done.

When choosing treats, try to select ones that are small, easy to handle, and won't make a mess. Avoid treats that are too crumbly or sticky, as they can create distractions or leave residue on your pet's fur.

You may also want to consider bringing a favorite toy or object that your pet loves. This can help to keep them engaged and provide some fun, interactive moments during the shoot.

If it rains on the day of your pet photoshoot, you will need to decide whether to reschedule the shoot or continue with it despite the weather. Here are a few things to consider:

Safety: If the rain is heavy or accompanied by thunderstorms, it may not be safe to proceed with the photoshoot.

Location: If your photoshoot is outdoors, rain may not be ideal for the kind of photos you have in mind.

Look and feel: Consider whether rain fits the look and feel you want for your photos. Some people love the dramatic, moody effect that rain can add to photos, while others prefer bright and sunny conditions.

Your pet's comfort: Rain can be uncomfortable for some pets, so make sure your furry friend is warm, dry, and comfortable throughout the shoot. Bring a towel, a dry blanket, or a raincoat to keep your pet dry and cozy during breaks.

Ultimately, the decision to reschedule or continue with the shoot is up to you. If you decide to proceed with the shoot, be sure to communicate with your photographer ahead of time to discuss any adjustments that may be necessary due to the weather.
